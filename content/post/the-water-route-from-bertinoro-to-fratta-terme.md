About two months ago, on April 4, 2026, I went on a short hike in the foothills of Romagna. The hike itself is easy and very enjoyable, but what made it special was the presence of two of our kids, Marco and Anna. 

We left the car in Fratta Terme, then crossed the one-and-a-half hills that lead to the beautiful village of Bertinoro, where we stopped on the famous piazza for a drink and to enjoy the renowned scenic view of the Pianura Padana and the Adriatic Sea. We got back to Fratta at sunset. 

{{< figure src="/images/the-waters-route.jpg" caption="With some effort, one can spot the medieval village of Bertinoro emerging from the hilltop on the left." >}}

I don’t know if I’ll have another opportunity to hike with my now very grown-up kids, so yes, despite its simplicity, this trip will always have a special place in my heart.

My GPX track is available on [Komoot](https://www.komoot.com/it-it/tour/2863173033). Another good resource is [The Waters Route](https://www.visitbertinoro.it/en/itinerari-new/via-delle-acque/) page on the Visit Bertinoro website.
